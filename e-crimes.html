TOP 10 tech-related Internet crimes


1. Phishing attacks

What is it?: According to Wikipedia, Phishing is the fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information or data, such as usernames, passwords and credit card details, by disguising oneself as a “trustworthy entity” in an electronic information. Typically carried out by email spoofing or Spam emails.


Digital Blackmailing

What is it?: It is the use of the internet maliciously, to threaten to cause any damage with the intent to extort from any person any belongings or valuables he/she may have. Such actions can be done through Social Networks, Emails, and other resources the suspect may use to threaten its victim.


3. Illegal access of stored communications

What is it?: The intention of accessing communication data stored in a server, without such authorization to do so. Such cybercrime is done via exploiting a server in a facility through which an electronic communication service is provided. It includes intercepting emails, messages, calls, and other communications, for the suspect/s’ advantage for malicious purposes.


4. E-sports betting

What is it?: An entity engaging on a business of betting or wagering on any sporting event and contests, that happens online. E-sports and gaming competitions can be used as an alternative to various cock fights and wagering businesses. Wagering ,in real life or over the internet is a federal crime.


5. Online shopping scams

What is it?: It is a scheme by cybercriminals in which they promise to deliver them their products and pretends to be a legitimate retailer. This kind of fraud is the most common crime in the world with almost every country by fraud retailers. The intention is to gather money by fooling the buyer into giving money to fake retailers and none in return because it is a scam.


6. Electronic Threatening

What is it?: The misuse of the internet to annoy, abuse or threaten their target who receives their communication. Targets of this cybercrime can be anyone the suspect/s’ know, or random people they come across the internet.


7. Plagiarism and abuse of Copyrights, piracy

What is it?: Using someone’s work for their own benefit and not giving the owner credits. The reuse of any content can be held liable on any demand of the owner. Forms of this crime includes copying and downloading of preowned files, documents, and other forms of work, done by the rightful owner, and selling, owning, and giving away of it is a strictly prohibited crime for adults and mostly students and children.


8. Distribution of any malware to networks and computers

What is it?: The distribution of malware is the infecting of computers using a written code. Its purpose is to destroy, steal, or infect more digital data. It can be prevented using any antivirus or antimalware programs to prevent the attack of a malware into computers.


9. Email bombing and robot call flooding

What is it?: It is the continuous sending of SPAM emails to the victim and flooding its inbox or spam folder with unnecessary emails. Its purpose is to send as many emails as possible to the target computer, to crash it or to overload its email and when the storage limit is full, it might cause charges to the user if not stopped. Call flooding is the use of a software that makes anonymous robot calls, to flood the telephone or phone with many call entries. This method works as it can cause charges to the user also, and it can also be used to annoy him/her with 100-1000 robot calls.


10. Hacking

What is it?: The most popular and common cybercrime since it is the most dangerous crime people have ever done on their computers. It works by having to get the username and the password of their desired target and using it for malicious purposes such as threatening for the hacker’s, own benefit. Malware attacks and Phishing are also types of hacking, it also has the same purpose which is to get the username and password of any protected accounts like banking accounts, Emails, Social Media accounts and more. Since it is the most popular cybercrime, there are many solutions and preventative measures to stop them.


Conclusion

In conclusion, these popular and common crimes can be seen without technology. The only difference is it has a different platform and different solutions to stop them. There are more cybercrimes that happen in almost every single day, people just don’t notice them. The advice to non-techy and techy type of people is to always watch out because everyone can be a victim and if preventative measures aren’t done, it could spread to others. Technology and the world is innovating and upgrading, so as the misuse of it. The four words to remember if was already attacked by cybercriminals is, WATCH OUT NEXT TIME.
